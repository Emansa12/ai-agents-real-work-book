\section{Introduction: When AI Agents Start Doing Real Work}

Organizations are adopting tool-enabled AI agents to complete work that previously required
handoffs between people and software. An agent can retrieve live information, draft outputs,
call APIs, and move a task through defined approval steps. That capability is different from
a chatbot that answers isolated prompts without owning an outcome \cite{mediumworkflowagents}.

\subsection{What AI Agents Are---and What They Are Not}

AI agents pursue goals across multiple steps inside a workflow. They combine language
reasoning with tools: search interfaces, document systems, ticketing platforms, and
automation connectors. Workforce research models this as task delegation---workers choose
which occupational steps to hand to software and which to keep under direct control
\cite{arxiv2506workagents}. Agents therefore belong in operations design, not only in
experimentation labs.

Chatbots optimize conversational fluency. Agents optimize \emph{task completion} within
supervised boundaries. The distinction matters when organizations evaluate automation
programs: a fluent answer is not the same as a completed research brief, resolved ticket,
or approved compliance checklist.

\subsection{Task-Level Automation, Not Total Job Replacement}

Live discussions about ``replacing real work'' often collapse two different ideas. Task
automation removes repeatable steps---data gathering, first-draft writing, routing, and
status updates. Role redesign reshapes who owns judgment, approval, and customer
relationships. Administrative automation studies show agents absorbing high-volume structured
tasks first while strategic decisions remain human-led \cite{recomaijobroles}.

This book uses \emph{replacement} in the task sense. Agents can remove manual effort from
specific workflows without making credible claims about eliminating entire professions.
Practitioner forums emphasize the same boundary: multi-step agents perform well when goals
and tools are explicit, but struggle when ambiguity, cross-domain judgment, or incomplete
tooling dominates \cite{redditcomplextasks}.

\subsection{Human Oversight Remains Central}

Human-in-the-loop review is not a temporary compromise. It is how organizations manage
risk as automation scope grows. Operators approve plans, validate evidence, and intercept
errors before external actions occur. Auditing frameworks for the future of work ask which
tasks employees trust agents to perform and which require persistent human accountability
\cite{arxiv2506workagents}. Sustainable programs encode those tiers as policy---observe,
approve, collaborate, or escalate---rather than assuming the model will self-correct under
pressure.

The chapters that follow examine agents and automation from complementary angles:

Together, these sections support a practical thesis: AI agents are reshaping how real work
gets done by automating bounded tasks inside supervised processes. The opportunity is
substantial when organizations match tool-enabled agents to the right work, keep humans on
consequential decisions, and measure outcomes by workflow quality rather than hype
\cite{mediumworkflowagents,recomaijobroles}.
